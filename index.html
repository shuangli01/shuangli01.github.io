About me

I am an Assistant Professor in the School of Data Science at The Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen. Previously, I was a postdoctoral fellow in the Statistical Reinforcement Learning Lab at Harvard University, advised by Prof. Susan Murphy. I received my Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from the H. Milton Stewart School of Industrial and Systems Engineering at Georgia Tech in 2019, advised by Prof. Yao Xie and Prof. Le Song. Before that, I earned a B.E. in Automation from the University of Science and Technology of China in 2011.

I’m recruiting Research Assistants year-round and have 1–2 PhD openings starting Fall 2027. If you are interested in working with me and have good programming skills and math background, you can contact me via email with your CV.

Research Interests

My research develops rule-guided sequential intelligence: reliable ML models that learn from sequences of events and decisions to predict, explain, and deliberate.

I combine temporal point processes, neuro-symbolic reasoning, cognitive modeling, and sequential decision-making to build interpretable AI for high-stakes domains such as healthcare, human-AI collaboration, choice modeling, and data-driven operations.
